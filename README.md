## Core Risk Themes Reflected in NERC CIP

NERC CIP broadly emphasizes control of risk in the following areas.

### Design Authority
- Who has authority to design systems that affect control, protection, or connectivity
- Clear ownership of architecture decisions
- Accountability for design choices that introduce exposure

### Access Control
- Limiting who can access systems
- Avoiding shared or unmanaged access
- Ensuring access is approved, known, and revocable

### External Connectivity
- Understanding what systems connect externally
- Identifying trust boundaries
- Controlling remote and vendor access paths

### Change Control
- Knowing when systems or configurations change
- Preventing unauthorized or undocumented changes
- Maintaining approved configurations

### Vendor and Supply Chain Risk
- Recognizing that third parties introduce risk
- Managing vendor access and design influence contractually
- Requiring transparency around third-party dependencies

### Evidence and Documentation
- Documented architecture and connectivity
- Retained records of approvals and changes
- Ability to demonstrate informed decisions

---

## Assumptions Reflected in CIP Intent

CIP assumes, at a minimum, that asset owners:
- Know who has access to systems
- Understand system boundaries and interfaces
- Control design decisions that affect exposure
- Retain documentation of architecture and changes
- Can remove access when relationships end

These assumptions inform contractual expectations.
